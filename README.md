This project examines whether starting at a two-year community college affects the total years of education a student completes. 
Using data from the High School and Beyond survey (4,437 students, class of 1980), three methods are applied: OLS regression with interaction terms,
Propensity Score Matching, and Random Forest feature importance, to answer three SMART research questions about the effect across Hispanic vs non-Hispanic students, 
low-income vs higher-income students, and which background factor matters most. Based on the original Rouse (1995) dataset.
